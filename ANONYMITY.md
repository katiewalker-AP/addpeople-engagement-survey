# How your anonymity is protected

**This is for staff communications — please feel free to share this with your team.**

---

## What we collect

When you submit the survey, we record:

- Your answers to the 13 questions
- The team you belong to (selected from a dropdown — no free-text names)
- The time you submitted
- A randomly generated ID (a string of random letters and numbers, not linked to you)

## What we do NOT collect

- Your name
- Your email address
- Your IP address
- Any browser fingerprint or device identifier
- Any information about which device or browser you used

## How submissions work

Your answers are sent directly to a Google Sheet. There is no login, no account creation, and no session that could be tied back to you. The random ID generated for each submission is created in your browser at the moment you click submit — it has no relationship to your identity.

## Who can see the results

The results dashboard is password-protected with Google OAuth and accessible only to a small number of named people in the People and Leadership teams. Individual responses are never shared — results are always shown as aggregated scores (team averages, company-wide trends).

## What happens with your feedback

Open-text responses (the "one thing you'd improve" question) are read by the People team in full, without any name attached. They are used to identify themes and inform real changes — not to identify individuals.

## A note on team sizes

To further protect anonymity, team-level breakdowns in the dashboard are only shown for teams with **2 or more responses**. Teams with fewer than 2 responses are hidden.
